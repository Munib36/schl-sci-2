CFCs & The Ozone

ChloroFluoroCarbon (CFC) is one of the Ozone-Depleting-Substances (ODS)

Why are CFCs harmful to our atmosphere?

CFCs slowly rise due to density, once they reach the stratosphere they get broken down by strong Ultra-Violet radiation from the sun and release harmful chlorine into the air

Montreal protocol

signed in Montreal, Canada. adopted on Sept, 16, 1987 by 197 countries; seeked to achieve protection of the stratosphere ozone layer, by reducing the consumption and production of ODS (Ozone-Depliting-Substances).
CFCs had record high levels in 2020 but fortunately its steadily declining now.

biggest produces of CFCs today are: China, South Korea, Russia and Venezuela
